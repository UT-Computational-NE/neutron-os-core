## 1. Research Program Overview

### 1.1 Active Projects

The following research thrusts are active in the current funding cycle and will directly
benefit from the requested compute allocation:

**Advanced Reactor Safety Analysis**
Thermal-hydraulic and neutronics simulation of advanced reactor concepts under normal and
off-normal conditions. These campaigns require large ensembles of coupled simulations across
design parameter spaces.

**Uncertainty Quantification for Legacy Fuel Performance**
Monte Carlo-based uncertainty propagation for fuel performance modeling. A single UQ study
may require O(10^3) independent simulation runs.

**Digital Twin Development**
Development and validation of real-time digital twin infrastructure for operating test reactors.
Validation requires high-fidelity simulation benchmarks that exceed on-campus cluster capacity.

### 1.2 Compute Demand Pattern

Workloads are episodic: quiet periods of local development punctuated by intensive simulation
campaigns tied to grant deliverables and conference submissions. Cloud resources are well-matched
to this pattern — they eliminate the queue delay that currently causes multi-week slippage in
deliverable timelines.

### 1.3 Current Constraints

The on-campus HPC cluster provides shared access across the department. During peak periods
(semester end, grant reporting cycles), queue wait times exceed two weeks for large jobs.
For time-sensitive deliverables, this is operationally blocking.

Cloud resources provide on-demand access without queue competition, enabling the team to
meet deliverable timelines reliably.
