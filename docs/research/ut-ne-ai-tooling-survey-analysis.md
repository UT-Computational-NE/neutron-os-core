# UT Nuclear Engineering AI Tooling Survey Analysis

**Survey:** UT Nuclear Engineering State of AI Tooling (5 Questions)  
**Respondents:** 12  
**Collection Period:** January 5–12, 2026  
**Analysis Date:** February 5, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

A survey of 12 UT Nuclear Engineering faculty, staff, and students reveals strong demand for premium AI tools beyond what UT currently provides. The data supports a focused investment in **Anthropic (Claude/Opus), OpenAI (Codex), and Google (Gemini)** access.

**Key finding:** 100% of respondents want ChatGPT access; 50% want Claude; 50% want Gemini. Current UT-provided tools (free Copilot) are explicitly cited as insufficient.

## Budget Request Justification

### What the Survey Supports

1. **Universal ChatGPT demand (100%)** — OpenAI access is baseline expectation
2. **Strong Claude demand (50%)** — Anthropic's Opus 4.5 enables advanced coding workflows
3. **Strong Gemini demand (50%)** — Google ecosystem integration valued
4. **High-intensity users exist** — 50% want 60+ hrs/month, justifying pro tiers
5. **Current tools insufficient** — free Copilot explicitly called out as inadequate
6. **Out-of-pocket spending occurring** — department should capture this productivity

### Current Request (~$10K/year)

The proposed budget covers:

- **Anthropic (Opus 4.5)** — advanced reasoning, Claude Code CLI
- **OpenAI (Codex)** — physics applications, code generation
- **Google (Gemini)** — ecosystem integration, Gemini CLI

This is a **trimmed subset** of the full survey wishlist, focused on the three platforms with highest demand and clearest differentiation.

### ROI Framing

If 6 heavy users (50% of respondents) gain even **2× productivity** (conservative vs. reported 10–100×) on 20% of their work time, the department recovers the $10K investment in **weeks, not months**.
